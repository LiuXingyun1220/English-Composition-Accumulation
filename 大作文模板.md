# 图画作文
## 首段（3-4句）
### 第1句：总体描述

【主语从句】
- What is (vividly) depicted in this drawing is such a (symbolic) scene: [句子].
-  （主语从句；对比）What is depicted in these drawings is `a stark contrast`: [句子,使用 while 或 whereas 连接的并列句].
	- What is depicted in these drawings is a stark contrast: in the first picture, a young man sits quietly in a library, completely **immersed in** the world of books, whereas the second picture shows another individual **glued to** his smartphone, looking anxious and overwhelmed by digital information.（“这些图画所描绘的是一个鲜明的对比：在第一幅图中，一个年轻人安静地坐在图书馆里，完全沉浸在书海中；然而第二幅图则展示了另一个人正（像被粘住一样）盯着他的智能手机，看起来既焦虑又被数字信息所淹没。”）
---
【倒装1】
- `Portrayed`/`Depicted`/`Shown` in the drawing is `such a scene` / `an (striking/thought-provoking/eye-catching) scene`: [句子，首字母大写].
- ---
【倒装2】
- Before us lies an illuminating scene: [句子].
- （分词短语倒装）Unfolding before us is an enlightening scene: [句子].
> illuminating adj. 富有启发性的 == enlightening

---
【标准陈述句型】
- 短句：A remarkable and significant image comes into view: [句子].
> remarkable adj. 非凡的
> significant adj. 意味深长的
- 长句：An extraordinary and meaningful drawing unfolds before our eyes: [句子].
### 第2-3句：图画细节描述
【单图】场景/中心人物(how, do what)/周边事物(how, do what)
1. 【地点状语倒装】In/On/At [地点] stand(s)/sit(s)/lie(s) a/an [中心人物], [v-ing/非谓语, 描述其动作、神态并自然地引出周边事物].
	- `At the center of the stage stands a Peking Opera actor, vividly performing a classic role, yet his audience consists of only a few scattered viewers.` (舞台中央站着一位京剧演员，他正生动地表演一个经典角色，然而他的观众却只有寥寥数人。)
	- `On a park bench sits a young man, completely absorbed in his smartphone, seemingly oblivious to the pictureque spring scenery around him.` (在公园长椅上坐着一个年轻人，他全神贯注地沉浸在智能手机里，似乎对周围如画的春景浑然不觉。)
2. 【分词倒装】Sitting/Lying/Standing in/on [地点] is a/an [中心人物], **[v-ing/非谓语, 描述其动作和周边事物]**.
	- `Lying on a hospital bed is a patient, tightly clutching a smartphone, while the doctor standing beside him looks on helplessly.` (躺在病床上的是一个病人，他紧紧抓着智能手机，而旁边的医生无奈地看着。)
---
【双图】在一个大背景下，【主人公1】以什么样的方式做了什么，而【主人公2】又做了什么
大背景[非谓语]，[主1谓宾，非谓语1], while [主2谓宾，非谓语2].
- Seeing the drink spill out, one person falls into despair, sighing, "All is over," while the other person swiftly lifts the bottle, saying, "I feel so lucky to still have something left in the bottle."
