1. **face the ax**（n. 斧头）  面临淘汰  `be fired, be cancelled`
	- In an era of rapid technological advancement, individuals who fail to engage in lifelong learning and update their skills may eventually **face the ax** in the competitive job market. (在一个技术飞速发展的时代，那些不能进行终身学习、更新技能的个人，在竞争激烈的就业市场中可能最终会**面临被淘汰的命运**。)
2. **survey**  v.审视，打量 `consider, review, assess, think about`
	- Before making a final decision about their career path, every graduate should take a moment to mentally **survey** the opportunities and adversity ahead, weighing the stability of a traditional job against the risks and rewards of doing pioneering work.(在对自己的职业道路做出最终决定前，每一位毕业生都应该花些时间，在脑海中**审度**前方的机遇与逆境，并权衡传统工作的稳定性与从事开创性工作所带来的风险和回报。)
3. **reckon with** (sth/sb)   ^918c94
	-  **（严肃）面对**（一个棘手的问题或强大的对手）`confront, contend with, grapple with`
		- As we embrace the convenience of AI, we must also **reckon with** the profound ethical dilemmas and potential job displacement it brings. (当我们在拥抱人工智能的便利时，我们也必须**严肃面对**它所带来的深刻的伦理困境和潜在的岗位流失。)
	- （**认真）考虑，估量**（常用于否定句）
		- The company failed **to reckon with** the shifting demands of its consumers, leading to its eventual bankruptcy. (这家公司未**能充分考虑到**其消费者需求的转变，最终导致了破产。)
		- Policymakers must **reckon with** the long-term needs of an aging population. (政策制定者必须**认真考虑**人口老龄化的长期需求。)
