1. **face the ax**（n. 斧头）  面临淘汰  `be fired, be cancelled`
	- In an era of rapid technological advancement, individuals who fail to engage in lifelong learning and update their skills may eventually **face the ax** in the competitive job market. (在一个技术飞速发展的时代，那些不能进行终身学习、更新技能的个人，在竞争激烈的就业市场中可能最终会**面临被淘汰的命运**。)
2. **survey**  v.审视，打量 `consider, review, assess, think about`
	- Before making a final decision about their career path, every graduate should take a moment to mentally **survey** the opportunities and adversity ahead, weighing the stability of a traditional job against the risks and rewards of doing pioneering work.(在对自己的职业道路做出最终决定前，每一位毕业生都应该花些时间，在脑海中**审度**前方的机遇与逆境，并权衡传统工作的稳定性与从事开创性工作所带来的风险和回报。)
3. **reckon with** (sth/sb)   ^918c94
	-  **（严肃）面对**（一个棘手的问题或强大的对手）`confront, contend with, grapple with`
		- As we embrace the convenience of AI, we must also **reckon with** the profound ethical dilemmas and potential job displacement it brings. (当我们在拥抱人工智能的便利时，我们也必须**严肃面对**它所带来的深刻的伦理困境和潜在的岗位流失。)
	- （**认真）考虑，估量**（常用于否定句）
		- The company failed **to reckon with** the shifting demands of its consumers, leading to its eventual bankruptcy. (这家公司未**能充分考虑到**其消费者需求的转变，最终导致了破产。)
		- Policymakers must **reckon with** the long-term needs of an aging population. (政策制定者必须**认真考虑**人口老龄化的长期需求。) 
4. **derive A from B**  从A中获取B; **draw A from B**  从A中汲取B;  `get A from B`
	- Young people should be encouraged to **derive** inspiration **from** their own traditional culture, rather than blindly imitating foreign trends. （应该鼓励年轻人**从**他们自己的传统文化中**汲取**灵感，而不是盲目模仿外国潮流。）
	- **Instead of yielding to** despair, we should learn to **draw strength and wisdom from** setbacks and failures. (我们不应向绝望屈服，而应学会**从**挫折和失败中**汲取**力量和智慧。)
5. **grip**
	- 描述负面现象的控制/束缚：  **have a tight grip on...**; **tighten its grip on...**; **be in the grip of...**
		- The fierce peer pressure in the job market **has a tight grip on** graduates, forcing many to take low-paying internships reluctantly. (就业市场激烈的同辈压力**牢牢地束缚着**毕业生，迫使许多人无奈接受低薪实习。) 
		- The trend of becoming Internet celebrities through short videos **has tightened its grip on** some teenagers, who even skip classes to film content. (通过短视频成为网红的趋势**对部分青少年的影响愈演愈烈**，他们甚至为了拍摄内容而逃课。)
		- Many young people today seem to **be in the grip of** anxiety about their uncertain future. (如今许多年轻人似乎**深受**对未来不确定性的焦虑**之苦**。)
	- 描述对正面价值观的坚守/动摇：  **keep a firm grip on**; **loosen one's grip on...**
		- In a world full of temptations, it is crucial for us to **keep a firm grip on** our moral principles and integrity. (在一个充满诱惑的世界里，我们**坚守**道德原则和诚信至关重要。)
		- The spread of negative information online is gradually **loosening citizens' grip on** positive values. (网络负面信息的传播正逐渐**弱化公民对正向价值观的坚守**。)
	- 描述吸引注意力/产生影响：  **grip(v.)**; **a gripping(adj.) story/image**
		- What **grips** our attention in the drawing is a young man struggling in a net of social media. (图画中**紧紧抓住我们眼球**（吸引我们注意）的是一个在社交媒体之网中挣扎的年轻人。)
		- This TV series vividly depicts the reality of family life, delivering a **gripping** story that resonates with audiences of all ages. (这部电视剧生动描绘了家庭生活的现实，讲述了一个**引人入胜的**故事，引起了各年龄段观众的共鸣。)
6. **be brimming with**  `be full of`
	- 形容人物的状态（自信、活力、希望）
		- Portrayed in the drawing is a young runner who **is brimming with** confidence and determination, ready to face any challenge. (画中描绘了一位**洋溢着**自信和决心的年轻跑者，准备好迎接任何挑战。)
	- 形容社会、市场或文化的活力
		- Thanks to the reform and opening-up policy, China's economy **is brimming with** vitality and opportunities. (得益于改革开放政策，中国经济**充溢着**活力与机遇。)
		- Traditional Chinese culture **is brimming with** timeless charm and wisdom. (中国传统文化**洋溢着**永恒的魅力与智慧。)
	- (偶尔用于) 形容负面事物的“充斥”
		- The virtual world, while convenient, **is also brimming with** false information and potential traps. (虚拟世界虽然便利，但也**充斥着**虚假信息和潜在陷阱。)
